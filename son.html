FPT Innovator Creates Survey Tool to Compete with SurveyMonkey and Google Forms

Friday, May 26, 2017 | 17:45 GMT+7

Previously, 20 people spent an entire month compiling statistics and sending emails, but now, with the automated system, one person can complete the task in minutes, saving over 90% of the effort.

Nguyen Thai Son, Deputy Head of FPT IS Quality Assurance Department and creator of the automated survey tool, which recently won the Bronze Prize in the February iKhien innovation contest, shared that he spent six months developing the tool. During that time, he took advantage of both company work and product research, working regardless of lunch breaks or "cutting" his family's time at night. His goal was to develop a fast and efficient survey tool that could be used on any device, including tablets and smartphones, across all browsers.

Working in the Quality Assurance (QA) Department means being closely involved in processes and end-of-project surveys for both customers and internal departments. Just counting external customers, FPT IS has more than 100 projects at the same time while each survey period on the company's internal services has up to thousands of surveys.

Previously, when the survey period came, the entire QA project team had to “work regardless”, mobilizing everyone to spend a whole month making reminder emails, statistics and summarizing the survey results. Not to mention that the surveys sent to customers were all done on paper or sent via email with a simple interface, which did not create a professional image for the company. There were times when customers complained that FPT IS’s project survey was too “peasant”.

Touched by his “self-esteem”, Mr. Son decided to research and launch his own survey tool. The first project to utilize the survey tool was the Quang Ninh e-Government Deployment with nearly 1,000 surveys across the districts and communes of the province and was highly appreciated by customers.

Mr. Nguyen Thai Son - Deputy of FPT IS Quality Assurance Department, author of the automatic survey product that just won the Bronze prize in the iKhien innovation contest in February.

Compared to popular tools like SurveyMonkey and Google Forms, which lack full automation and require manual intervention when sending emails; Each survey in the same batch only uses one language and the questionnaires must be completely identical in terms of questions; Those tools also cannot automatically classify the number and identity of unanswered surveys... Mr. Thai Son's automatic survey can completely do these things.

With automatic surveys, the system has a set of surveys available including: Invitation mail, reminder mail, thank you mail, feedback mail and survey form.

When the survey date comes, the tool will use “Invitation Mail” to send the first time. The next times, if the customer has not done it yet, the system will automatically use “Mail reminder” to send it again until the customer does it or the schedule is over. Instead of 20 people doing statistics and sending emails for the whole month, now only one person can do the operations in just a few minutes, not counting the time to check the list of survey subjects, saving more than 90% of the effort.

"Thanks to the survey tool, everything is automated, helping QA reduce a lot of time and effort in customer surveys. However, to perfect the tool, there needs to be some improvements to the product such as: Every 6 months, there will be an email reminding the QA in charge of the survey that is about to expire, instead of manually filtering from an Excel file as it is now; The interface needs to add a logo to know FPT products...", Ms. Nguyen Thi Thuy Hang, QA FPT IS, commented.

Mr. Thai Son excitedly said that there have been customers outside of FPT ordering this survey tool. It is predicted that the potential market for the product will expand to customers in the Insurance, Education and Banking industries.

He noted that while FPT IS has many initiatives aimed at improving work efficiency, they often remain unknown or are applied only in small groups. This iKhien contest is an opportunity for individuals to widely introduce their products to many people, and more importantly, to stimulate the creativity of FPT people.

Born in 1974, Nguyen Thai Son graduated from Ho Chi Minh City University of Economics with a degree in Information Technology. He joined FPT in 1997 and has been with the company ever since.

As a student, he won the second prize in the National Informatics Competition and was the “chairman” of the Informatics Club at school. Mr. Thai Son is also famous for the project “CD - Saigon 300 years” which was a graduation thesis commercialized by FPT and thanks to using Microsoft technology to develop CD, he was personally invited by Bill Gates to Singapore to attend the new technology announcement ceremony. Thanks to this famous product, the CD project team was honored as one of the 20 factors contributing the most to Vietnam in IT, published in the book “The Flame of Youth” - Tre Publishing House 1998.

At FPT, Mr. Thai Son was the first Secretary of the Youth Union of FPT HCM (the predecessor of the General Association), he was also one of the people who laid the foundation and built the Group Management System to obtain ISO certification. He participated in a series of large projects such as: Online business registration of the Department of Planning and Investment, People's Committee of District 1, Trieu An Hospital, Vietsovpetro (VSP)... and was the Head of the Enterprise Software Department of FSS (later merged with FPT IS). When the company needed to make a CMMi certification system, he transferred to QA to work until FSS, ERP and FPT IS merged into FPT IS, from 2007 to present. He is currently the Deputy Head of FPT IS Quality Assurance Department.

"iKhien" is a program that searches for and honors practical innovations that go into long-term operation and encourages the spirit of innovation and creativity within the group.

Participants are all FPT employees (can participate as individuals or groups). The program lasts from February 13 to December 13. The deadline for submission of creative works will be on the 13th of each month. Register your creativity here.
